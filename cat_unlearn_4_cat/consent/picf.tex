\subsection{Introduction}
My name is Dr Matthew J Crossley and I am a Senior Lecturer
in the School of Psychological Sciences at Macquarie
University. Data collection may be carried out by supervised
student researchers working under my oversight.  You are
invited to take part in this research project. This
Participant Information and Consent Form explains what the
project is about and what taking part would involve. Please
read it carefully before deciding whether to participate.

\subsection{Research team for this study session}
This project is led by Dr Matthew J Crossley (please see
contact details at the top of this form). Data for this
study session may be collected by the following supervised
student researcher(s):

\begin{itemize}
\item Eliza James, B Psych Hons, eliza.james2@students.mq.edu.au
\end{itemize} 

These student researcher(s) are conducting this research to
meet the requirements of the degree(s) listed above, under
the supervision of Dr Matthew J Crossley. Only researchers
listed in this section will collect your data in this
session.

\subsection{What is the purpose of this project?}
This research aims to help us better understand how people
learn, make decisions, and perform simple actions in
response to visual or auditory information. If you take
part, you will be contributing to a series of related
behavioural experiments about cognition, action, and
learning.

\subsection{Am I eligible to take part?}
To take part in this study, you must:
\begin{itemize}
\item be at least 18 years old;

\item be fluent enough in English to understand the
    instructions and consent materials;

\item be able to complete the study online or attend an
    in-person testing session;

\item have normal or corrected-to-normal vision and hearing
    with your usual corrective aids; and

\item have sufficient motor function to press keyboard
    buttons, click or move a mouse or trackpad, or make
    another similar response.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Where is this study taking place?}
This study may be conducted online or in person in dedicated
lab space at Macquarie University. This Participant
Information and Consent Form applies to both online and
in-person participation. If you are participating in person,
you will provide consent first and then complete the task on
a computer in the lab. If you are participating online, you
will be directed to Pavlovia
(\url{https://www.pavlovia.org}) for the behavioural task
after providing consent here.

\subsection{What will I be asked to do?}
If you agree to participate, you will be asked to complete
one or more simple computer-based tasks. These tasks involve
repeated trials in which stimuli are presented on a computer
screen and you respond by pressing keys, clicking or moving
a mouse or trackpad, or making another similar response.
Some tasks include sounds presented at a comfortable volume.

You may also be asked to provide a small amount of
background information, such as your age, gender,
handedness, and gaming experience, as well as brief ratings
or comments about your strategy, effort, or task difficulty.

Most sessions will involve:
\begin{itemize}
\item about 10 minutes to read this information sheet and
    provide consent; and

\item about 30 minutes for the behavioural task, with total
    task time falling within 30 to 50 minutes.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{What information will be collected?}
If you take part, we will collect the following information:

\begin{itemize}
\item recruitment and administrative information such as
    your name, email address, phone number, participant ID,
    or platform ID, where needed for scheduling, course
    credit, or payment;

\item consent records, including your explicit consent
    confirmation in a university-approved survey tool;

\item coded behavioural data such as responses, response
    times, movement times, trial information, and
    experimental condition; and

\item brief demographic and task-related information such as
    age, gender, handedness, gaming experience, strategy
    reports, and difficulty or effort ratings.
\end{itemize}

\Needspace{18\baselineskip}
\subsection{How will my information be stored and protected?}
We will handle your information as confidentially as possible.

\begin{itemize}
\item If you take part in person at Macquarie University,
    your behavioural data will be collected under a random
    participant code and stored on secure, dedicated lab
    computers.

\item If you take part online, your behavioural data will be
    collected under a random participant code and stored on
    Pavlovia/GitLab infrastructure.

\item Data from lab computers and Pavlovia/GitLab will be
    exported to secure analysis computers for analysis.

\item Identifying information (for example, consent,
    recruitment, and payment records) is stored separately
    from behavioural research data in secure,
    university-approved systems. These records are retained
    for at least five years from acquisition and then
    securely destroyed.

\item Identifying information may also be stored in
    recruitment platforms (for example, MQ SONA, Prolific,
    or Mechanical Turk) for enrolment, credit, or payment
    processing under those platforms' systems. Access is
    limited to authorised research team members.

\item Behavioural data will not contain information that
    directly identifies you. The research team does not keep
    a linkage key between consent records and behavioural
    datasets, so it cannot link behavioural data to your
    identifying information.

\item Any datasets or study materials shared publicly will
    not contain information that directly identifies you.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{What happens to my information if I ask for it to be removed?}
If you withdraw before finishing the task, no further data
will be collected from you. If you ask for your data to be
removed, we will delete research data where this is
technically possible. In practice, this is usually only
possible during an in-person session, or immediately after,
while we can still tell which active file is yours.
Behavioural task data are stored under a random code, and
the research team does not keep a linkage key between
consent records and behavioural datasets. Once time has
passed, we may no longer be able to tell which file is
yours, so deletion may not be possible. Consent records are
kept separately as study governance records.

\subsection{How will my information be used?}
What we learn from this research may be published in journal
articles, conference presentations, theses, and other
scholarly outputs. De-identified behavioural data, analysis
code, and study materials may also be shared through
research repositories such as OSF, GitHub, or GitLab to
support transparency and reproducibility. Records that
identify you, including consent and recruitment records,
will not be publicly shared. Results may also be included in
student theses or dissertations for the degree programs
listed in this form.

\subsection{Will I be told the results of the research?}
If you would like a summary of the overall findings, you may
contact the research team by email. We will not be able to
provide you with individual results because the research
data are coded (de-identified) and the research team does not
keep a linkage key between consent records and behavioural
datasets.

\subsection{Are there any risks or discomforts?}
The main foreseeable risks are mild frustration, boredom, or
temporary concern about your performance if you find a task
difficult. Finding the task difficult does not mean there is
anything wrong with you. You are free to take a break
whenever you want, and you can stop the study at any time
without giving a reason and without penalty. If you become
upset or uncomfortable, the study can be stopped straight
away.

\subsection{Are there any benefits?}
You should not expect a direct personal benefit from taking
part. However, the research may contribute to a better
scientific understanding of human learning, cognition, and
action.

\Needspace{12\baselineskip}
\subsection{Will I be paid?}
Whether you are paid will depend on how you were recruited
for the particular study.

\begin{itemize}
\item If you were recruited through Prolific or Mechanical
    Turk, you will be paid at a rate of \$15 AUD per hour,
    or pro rata.

\item If you were recruited through the Macquarie University
    Psychology participant pool, you will receive course
    credit. For online participation, you will receive 1
    credit for every 30 minutes of participation. For
    on-campus participation, you will receive 1.5 credits
    for every 30 minutes of participation.

\item If you were recruited in another way, you may be
    entered into a draw to receive one of three \$150 AUD Coles
    vouchers, if this was stated in the recruitment
    materials.
\end{itemize}

If you withdraw after starting, you will not be penalised.
If you are being paid for participation, you will be paid
pro rata for the time you have completed. If you are
participating for course credit, you will receive the course
credit described in the recruitment materials even if you
stop early. If you are participating for entry into the
Coles voucher draw, you will remain in the draw even if you
withdraw after starting.

\subsection{Do I have to take part?}
No. Participation is completely voluntary. If you decide not
to take part, there will be no penalty or disadvantage to
you. If you decide to take part and then change your mind,
you may withdraw at any time without giving a reason and
without penalty.
